# Business Plan Writing Standards

## Purpose
These rules ensure that all business plan documents maintain professional quality, strategic clarity, and investor-readiness. Apply these standards across all generated content.

## Tone and Voice

- **Professional yet Accessible**: Write for educated business readers without domain expertise
- **Confident but Honest**: Assert strengths without overstating; acknowledge challenges
- **Data-Driven**: Support claims with specific examples, metrics, and research
- **Future-Oriented**: Frame the business as positioned for future growth and opportunity

## Structure and Clarity

### Headings Hierarchy
```
# Executive Level (H1) - Major sections
## Topic Level (H2) - Subsections
### Detail Level (H3) - Specific points
```

### Paragraph Guidelines
- **Opening Sentence**: States the main idea clearly
- **Length**: 3-5 sentences per paragraph (scannable)
- **Evidence**: Each claim includes supporting data or example
- **Transitions**: Clear connections between ideas

## Key Sections: Writing Principles

### Executive Summary
- **Length**: 1-2 pages maximum
- **Content**: Problem, solution, market opportunity, competitive advantage, financials snapshot
- **Tone**: Compelling but credible
- **Rule**: Must be understandable by someone with 10 minutes to read

### Market Analysis
- **Specificity**: Use exact market sizes, growth rates, and sources
- **Segments**: Clearly identify addressable market segments
- **Trends**: Connect to broader industry trends and megatrends
- **Rule**: Make TAM/SAM/SOM calculations explicit

### Competitive Analysis
- **Balance**: Acknowledge strengths of competitors while positioning differentiation
- **Specifics**: Name actual competitors and their approaches
- **Positioning**: Make clear why you're different and defensible
- **Rule**: Never dismiss competitors; instead show why you solve problems better

### Product/Service Description
- **Clarity**: Explain what you do in one sentence, then elaborate
- **Benefits**: Focus on client outcomes, not just features
- **Differentiation**: Highlight what makes your approach superior
- **Rule**: Answer "Why would a client choose this?" not just "What is this?"

## Language Principles

### Avoid
- Jargon without explanation
- Vague claims ("best," "leading," "innovative" without context)
- Passive voice when active would be clearer
- Contradictions or unsupported leaps

### Embrace
- Specific metrics and examples
- Active voice for clarity
- Concrete outcomes and results
- Logical flow and clear causation

## Formatting

### Lists
- Use bullet points for 3+ items
- Parallel structure (all sentences or all fragments)
- Maximum 5-7 items per list (break up longer lists)

### Emphasis
- **Bold** for key terms and conclusions
- *Italics* for emphasis, not overused
- Headings to break up dense sections

### Tables
- Use for comparisons and complex data
- Clear headers and labels
- Consistent formatting

- **Growth Rates**: Specify time period (e.g., "CAGR 2023-2026")
- **Sources**: Cite research and data sources where possible
- **Rounding**: Round appropriately; don't claim false precision

## Investor-Ready Standards

### Credibility
- Demonstrate deep market knowledge
- Show realistic understanding of challenges
- Include team credentials and relevant experience
- Use external validation (partnerships, pilot results, customer testimonials)

### Actionability
- Clear ask (what are you raising, what will you do with it?)
- Specific milestones and timelines
- Realistic but ambitious projections
- Risk awareness and mitigation strategies

### Professionalism
- No typos, grammatical errors, or formatting inconsistencies
- Consistent terminology throughout
- Professional document design and layout
- Clear visual hierarchy

## Specific to Forward Deployed AIX

### Market Positioning Language
- Emphasize **embedded expertise** and real-time problem-solving
- Highlight **context-aware** and **customized** nature of solutions
- Reference **operational integration** and **long-term partnerships**
- Focus on **client outcomes** and **measurable ROI**

### Competitive Differentiation
- Position vs. consulting (deeper technical integration)
- Position vs. software platforms (human expertise + software)
- Position vs. in-house teams (faster deployment, specialized expertise, managed services)

### Customer Value
- **Time to Value**: Faster deployment than alternatives
- **Risk Mitigation**: Expert oversight and proven methodologies
- **Compliance**: Security and regulatory compliance built-in
- **Scalability**: Grow AI implementation without proportional cost increases
