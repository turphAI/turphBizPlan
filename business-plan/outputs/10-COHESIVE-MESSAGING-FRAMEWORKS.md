# COHESIVE MESSAGING FRAMEWORKS: Title, Description, Tag, Mission

## Overview

This document presents complete messaging frameworks for each Category 1 business tag option. Each framework includes all 4 components working together as one cohesive message:

1. **Personal Title** - How you identify professionally
2. **Short LinkedIn Description** - What you actually do (1-2 sentences)
3. **Business Tag** - Core identifier
4. **Short Mission Statement** - Core purpose/value proposition

### Business Tag
**Workflow Intelligence**

### Personal Title Options

- Founder, Workflow Intelligence
- Consultant, Workflow Intelligence

### LinkedIn Description
**Option A (Outcome-focused):**
"Principal at Workflow Intelligence | I help enterprises discover workflow improvements by embedding with teams, understanding what they're actually trying to accomplish, then reimagining how agentic solutions can reshape task completion. 4-5 weeks instead of 6-12 months."

**Option B (Method-focused):**
"Principal at Workflow Intelligence | Embedded workflow prototyping for enterprise teams. I understand underlying tasks, reimagine how agentic solutions can complete them differently, and validate with rapid prototyping‚Äîall in 4-5 weeks."

### Mission Statement
"Workflow Intelligence helps enterprises discover what actually works in their workflows by embedding with teams, understanding underlying tasks, and reimagining how agentic solutions can reshape task completion‚Äîvalidated in weeks, not months."

### How They Work Together
- **Tag** positions you as intelligence-focused (data-driven, analytical)
- **Title** establishes credibility (Principal/Founder = expertise)
- **Description** explains the embedded, task-reimagining approach
- **Mission** reinforces the value: discovery through understanding, not digitization

### Pros
- "Intelligence" suggests data-driven, analytical approach
- Professional, credible sound
- Opens conversation ("What is workflow intelligence?")
- Differentiates from UX/design labels
- Memorable and clear

### Cons
- "Intelligence" might sound abstract or tech-focused
- Could be confused with business intelligence/analytics
- Less action-oriented than some alternatives
